<p>I run a small Web server. It's a vanity project, very low traffic - less than 50 pages served per day. Strictly HTML - no CGI, no PHP, nothing.  For the past couple of weeks the server has been the target of a SYN Flood attack. Also very low volume - 100-150 SYN packets (with forged IP addresses) received per hour. I have all the usual mitigations in place and the attack is not affecting the server.  However, I am curious why it's being attacked. I have not received any "ransom" email, the server does not host anything that is even mildly controversial and the forged IP addresses are always different and from all over the world so I'm not being used to flood some other server. Can anyone suggest a purpose for this attack?</p>
