Open world design and linear design both offer radically different kinds of experiences, and there’s no shortage of reasons to love either of them- but you know what we really appreciate? 

A game that properly manages to capture the strengths of both those styles. Semi-open world games – the good ones, at the very least – get to have their cake and it eat it too, because not only do they let players experience tightly designed and linear gameplay sequences, but they also have plenty of room for the kind of exploration that purely linear games tend to lack.
