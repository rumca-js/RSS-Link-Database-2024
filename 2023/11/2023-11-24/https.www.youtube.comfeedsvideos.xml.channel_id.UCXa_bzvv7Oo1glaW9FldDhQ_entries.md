There’s obviously undeniable appeal to any game that takes players to a richly imagined and immaculately realized fictional world with its own rules, but there’s also no doubting the impact that a game can have by transporting you to a virtual recreation of a place that exists (or existed) in the real world. 

That can come in many different forms – from games that strive to be authentic recreations of certain locations to those that take creative liberties in their takes on real-world places – and when done right, there’s a great deal of enjoyment to be had in those open world settings. 

To that end, here, we’re going to talk about a few standout open world games that have taken us to real places.
