## A quantum coherent spin in hexagonal boron nitride at ambient conditions
 - [https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01887-z](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01887-z)
 - RSS feed: https://www.nature.com/nmat/current_issue/rss
 - date published: 2024-05-20T10:09:37.266830+00:00

<p>Nature Materials, Published online: 20 May 2024; <a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01887-z">doi:10.1038/s41563-024-01887-z</a></p>Quantum coherent control of single-photon-emitting defect spins have been reported in hexagonal boron nitride, revealing that spin coherence is mainly governed by coupling to a few proximal nuclei and can be prolonged by decoupling protocols.

## Metal bond strength regulation enables large-scale synthesis of intermetallic nanocrystals for practical fuel cells
 - [https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01901-4](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01901-4)
 - RSS feed: https://www.nature.com/nmat/current_issue/rss
 - date published: 2024-05-20T10:09:34.499875+00:00

<p>Nature Materials, Published online: 20 May 2024; <a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01901-4">doi:10.1038/s41563-024-01901-4</a></p>Although structurally ordered intermetallic nanocrystals are promising electrocatalysts for fuel cells, their high-temperature large-scale preparation has proved challenging. A low-melting-point-metal-induced bond strength weakening strategy to promote alloy catalyst ordering is now proposed.
