<p>Pitched as Braveheart meets A Song of Ice and Fire, Scott Palmer’s A Memory of Song is a sweepingly epic yet intimately character-driven Norse-flavoured fantasy that will make any dark fantasy lover’s heart sing with both joy and sorrow. As with any good first instalment in an ambitiously epic fantasy series, A Memory of Song has a lot of moving pieces. Set in a bleak world that has lost its basic natural elements due to an ancient order of warlocks singing songs composed of blood magic, we follow two broken souls who are both on their own quest to seek vengeance and to protect their loved ones, no matter the cost. On the one hand, there’s Wulfee, the fierce and fearsome leader of the sacred Feldarra clan who is hell-bent on getting revenge on her estranged husband after losing her sons.
