## Archaeologists uncover the lives of WWII refugees in an underground quarry in northwestern France

During the Allied bombings from June 6 to late July 1944, hundreds of Caen residents took refuge in the Saingt quarry. Now abandoned and largely intact, it is a playground for archeologists, who have turned it into a conservation space and a laboratory for their methods.
