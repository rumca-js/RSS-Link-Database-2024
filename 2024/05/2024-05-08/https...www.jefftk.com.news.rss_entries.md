## Experience Switching to Right Shoulder Round

<p><span>

Contra dance has a figure where two people walk a small circle looking
at each other.  When it was introduced into contra in the 1970s as a
borrowing from ECD, it had the name "gypsy", originally </span>

<a href="https://www.jefftk.com/p/history-of-the-term-gypsy">from
Morris dancing</a>, but many communities now use "right shoulder
round".



<p>

In many dance communities the debate over whether and how to switch
functioned as a highly acrimonious culture war outlet.  I really
didn't want our group going through that, but talking publicly about
how I didn't want that at the time would have been counterproductive.
Now that it's been ~5y since switching to "right shoulder round" and
~10y from the first big online discussions, I think this is probably
something I can share some history on.

</p>
