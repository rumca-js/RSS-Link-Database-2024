<p>Intel’s latest “Arrow Lake” processor wasn’t up to snuff. On Friday, a key executive promised to outline went wrong, and to fix it. </p>



<p>On a <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P2OHRH7221w" data-type="link" data-id="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P2OHRH7221w">podcast with Hot Hardware</a>, Intel vice president and general manager Robert Hallock acknowledged that the Arrow Lake launch “didn’t go as planned.” That was evidenced in <a href="https://www.pcworld.com/article/2499641/core-ultra-9-285k-review-10-must-know-facts-intel-new-desktop-cpus.html">PCWorld’s Arrow Lake review</a> and reviews by others.</p>



<p>Hallock was frank about what Intel needed to do: explain what went wrong, either by the end of November or early December, and then work to fix it.</p>
