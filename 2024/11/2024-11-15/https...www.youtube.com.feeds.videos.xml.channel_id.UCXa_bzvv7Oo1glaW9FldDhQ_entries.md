The years between 2000 and 2009 are upheld by many as the pinnacle of single player games. Whether you agree with this notion or not, there are elements in many modern games that are shaped by multiplayer. 

Single player campaigns – if the game harbours an online component – tend not to be a developer’s primary focus. Whilst a handful of the games on this rundown feature online components they can still be viewed ostensibly as single player games first and foremost, and wow, what an era of games to pluck just fifteen titles from. 

The breadth of games during this period elicited innovative gameplay and newfound emotional depth of storytelling (or, in many cases a blend of both). Also of note is how well graphics from this period hold up to modern standards.
