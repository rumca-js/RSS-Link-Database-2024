A kulhad is a biodegradable, disposable clay cup used in India by food vendors to serve hot beverages like chai and some desserts like yogurt. The cups have been excavated from the Indus Valley from as far back as the end of the Stone Age, 5,000 years ago. They are generally unglazed and unpainted so the hot liquid will seep into the clay, which is said to affect the flavor of the beverages giving it a more earthy note. While synthetic materials like plastic and paper have started replacing these clay cups as they are cheaper and more convenient for vendors to acquire, the clay cups still persist in India today. The cost per kulhad can vary but generally sits around 1 rupee, or .01 USD. Each kulhad is made by hand in harsh conditions that take a significant toll on the artisans. But proponents point to kulhads as the healthier, more environmentally friendly option, though the science isn't all there.
