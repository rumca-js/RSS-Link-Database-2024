<p>If you’re a heavy user of Nvidia’s GeForce Now cloud gaming platform, you might want to make a note of its biggest new change.</p>



<p>Starting in 2025, users (including those who pay for premium service) will be limited to <strong>100 hours of streaming per month</strong>. While that’s a fairly high limit, it still bites hard versus the unlimited playtime that the subscription has allowed up until now.</p>



<p>If you’re currently a paying user, or if you sign up for a paid GeForce Now subscription between now and December 31, you’ll be <strong>grandfathered into unlimited streaming until 2026</strong>. (Just don’t let that subscription lapse.) Otherwise, if you go over the streaming limit and still want to continue playing, you can buy additional game time in 15-hour chunks for $3 (standard tier) or $6 (ultimate tier).</p>
