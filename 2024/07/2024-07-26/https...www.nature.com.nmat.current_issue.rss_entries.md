## Atomically precise inorganic helices with a programmable irrational twist
 - [https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01963-4](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01963-4)
 - RSS feed: https://www.nature.com/nmat/current_issue/rss
 - date published: 2024-07-26T10:00:41.544967+00:00

<p>Nature Materials, Published online: 26 July 2024; <a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01963-4">doi:10.1038/s41563-024-01963-4</a></p>Helical motifs in dense inorganic solids have remained exceedingly scarce. Now a type of 1D van der Waals helical crystal, GaSeI, is presented that manifests the rare quasi-periodic Boerdijk–Coxeter helix motif.

## Non-equilibrium transport in polymer mixed ionic–electronic conductors at ultrahigh charge densities

<p>Nature Materials, Published online: 26 July 2024; <a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01953-6">doi:10.1038/s41563-024-01953-6</a></p>Mixed ionic–electronic transport in conducting polymers remains poorly understood. Here the authors observe non-equilibrium electronic transport when counterions are unable to equilibrate in response to gate-injected electronic carriers.
