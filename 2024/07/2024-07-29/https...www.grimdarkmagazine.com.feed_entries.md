<p>HOUSE OF THE DRAGON 2&#215;3 &#8220;The Burning Mill&#8221; is the third episode of the second season. Unfortunately, it is also House of the Dragon&#8217;s first misstep after two exceptionally strong opening episodes. House of the Dragon is always at its best when it is dealing with the subtle characterization and politicking going on behind the scenes versus the big epic events. One of the reasons that season stumbled as often as it ran. However, this seems to be an episode that pauses for characterization that doesn&#8217;t really make sense in the context of the larger narrative. The story begins with a well-loved element from the books with the Bracken and Blackwoods families. These two were at each other&#8217;s throats for millennia in the background of Westeros and were shown doing it the previous season.
