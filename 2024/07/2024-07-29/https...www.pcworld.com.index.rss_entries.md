<p>Computers used to be a lot noisier than they are today. And I don’t just mean the whir of spinning hard disks and the rattle of fans—they used to boop and beep every time you turned them on.</p>



<p>If you miss the chimes of classic PC hardware but aren’t willing to switch to a machine that gets fewer than 1,000 frames per second in <em>Doom</em>, then this M.2 trinket might be for you.</p>



<p>Japanese online shop <a href="https://www.kadenken.com/view/item/000000001796">Kadenken</a> is selling an M.2 module with an integrated speaker that emits <a href="https://www.youtube.com/shorts/_09fWLpSQ9U">the distinctive beep-boops of an NEC PC-9800 series</a>, which was first sold in Japan in 1982.</p>
