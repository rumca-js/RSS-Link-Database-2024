<p>If you’re trying to scam someone, you want to reach as many people as you can — that’s why you’ve almost certainly received one of the <em>literally billions</em> of scam phone calls made every year.</p>



<p>But it’s not just phone calls you need to be cautious of. Scammers and fraudsters are no strangers to putting up fake websites, and one recent example involves sites impersonating Disney+.</p>



<p>This particular crop of phony websites is concerning because <a href="https://www.malwarebytes.com/blog/scams/2024/10/fake-disney-activation-page-redirects-to-pornographic-scam">Malwarebytes found them</a> ranking high in Google searches. That makes them particularly dangerous to anyone who doesn’t manually type the full <code>www.disneyplus.com</code> URL into the browser address bar and instead relies on a quick search to find the relevant web page.</p>
