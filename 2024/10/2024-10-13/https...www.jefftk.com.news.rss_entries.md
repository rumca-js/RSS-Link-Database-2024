<p><span>

In general I'd like to see a lot more of people writing about their
failures in addition to their successes.  If a bunch of people all try
a thing and have mixed results, and only the people with good results
write about it, people who don't know about this selection bias or
don't realize its extent are going to end up with overly positive
views.  I've </span>

<a href="https://www.jefftk.com/p/failed-vegan-choux-experiments">written</a>


<a href="https://www.jefftk.com/p/loft-bed-fan-guard">about</a> 

<a href="https://www.jefftk.com/p/a-strange-ach-corner-case">some</a> 

<a href="https://www.jefftk.com/p/failed-adventures-in-delay">of</a>


<a href="https://www.jefftk.com/p/when-i-left-google">my</a> 

<a href="https://www.jefftk.com/p/disappointing-table-refinishing">mistakes</a>, and I
think it would be good if this were a higher fraction of my posts.
