<p>To Cage a God is the latest adult fantasy offering from Sunday Times bestselling author Elizabeth May. It is a Slavic inspired fantasy steeped with gods, magic, and some romance. It has some dark elements which I think might appeal to the Grimdark Magazine crowd, and although overall I enjoyed reading To Cage a God, it is not one of my standout favourite reads of the year so far. In To Cage a God our main focus is on two sisters, Galina and Sera, who as a result of their mother’s horrific experimentation on them as children, each now houses a violent god inside them. Both women are able to access their gods’ powers, but pay an increasingly higher price each time they do. Not to mention that this caging of gods is only meant to happen to the ruling classes so Galina and Sera are in a permanent fugitive state.
