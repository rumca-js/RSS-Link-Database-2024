Natasha Lyonne, Elizabeth Olsen and Carrie Coon are magnificent and touching as siblings saying goodbye to their dying father, while Mike Leigh’s rage powers his movie about the 1819 massacre 
In Azazel Jacobs’s charged drama, three sisters gather at their father’s New York flat to watch over him as he lies dying. Rachel (Natasha Lyonne), who lives there, spends her time betting on sports and smoking weed, which sets off prickly elder sister Katie (Carrie Coon) and puts Elizabeth Olsen’s more pacific Christina into the role of peacemaker. With the camera rarely leaving the apartment’s few rooms, it’s quite a theatrical setup – a hothouse of delayed grief, sibling resentment and fragile coping strategies.
