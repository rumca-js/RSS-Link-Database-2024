Toronto film festival: the documentarian makes his hit-and-miss narrative debut with help from Tilda Swinton and Michael Shannon as privileged parents in a luxury bunker
The singing isn’t very good in The End but that’s not really a bad thing.
Joshua Oppenheimer’s intermittently fascinating and exhausting post-apocalyptic musical, starring Tilda Swinton and Michael Shannon, is about a family sheltered by their own insidious privilege, trying to maintain a cheery disposition despite the grim circumstances that surround them. The world has essentially burned to the ground. Shannon’s former energy sector executive minimizes the role he played in that.
