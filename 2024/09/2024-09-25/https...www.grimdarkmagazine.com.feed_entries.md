## REVIEW: The Boodless Princes by Charlotte Bond

<p>The Bloodless Princes feels like a very different novella compared to to The Fireborne Blade, with a move away from the focus on dragons and their magic and towards the magic of human mages and the battle between the two lords of the underworld (the gravelands). The mage Hosh attempts to swap his place in the gravelands with Saralene by taking over her body. In a desperate bid to save her partner, Saralene&#8217;s champion knight, Sir Maddileh, finds a very unlikely ally&#8211;one who was assumed to be dead. Together they must venture to the gravelands to request the aid of one of the Bloodless Princes, the rulers of the gravelands with one controlling the day and the other the night. Bond continues to showcase the more experimental allowances that I feel come with writing shorter form fiction. The Bloodless Princes could almost be a stand alone novella, the feel of it is so different to The Fireborne Blade, and I think that&#8217;s a good thing.
