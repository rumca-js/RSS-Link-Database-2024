<p>Aaron M. Payne comes blazing onto the scene full throttle with Falling Into Oblivion, a dark and electrifying cyberpunk murder mystery with an air of danger, urgency, and intrigue that is so addictive, it should honestly be criminal. It’s giving a bit of Dresden Files mixed with Neuromancer set against the backdrop of a neon-infused version of Batman’s Gotham City, and yet it is somehow also totally its own unique thing. Through the eyes of local police detective Sol Harkones, we are sucked into the dark and deceptively alluring underworld of Nox City where corruption and danger lurk around every grimy corner. After another unrewarding day of work, he wants nothing more than to go home to read his young daughter her bedtime story, but his latest job turns out to be something altogether more elaborate and sinister.
