##  Academic publishers hit with antitrust suit over Peer review
 - [http://www.bookbrowse.com/news/detail/index.cfm?news_item_number=3286](http://www.bookbrowse.com/news/detail/index.cfm?news_item_number=3286)
 - RSS feed: https://www.bookbrowse.com/rss/book_news.rss
 - date published: 2024-09-18T06:00:00+00:00

 Peer review&#8212;the process by which academics evaluate new manuscripts involving new research&#8212;has long been a cornerstone of the academic publishing process. But are large commercial publishers exploiting the peer review process for their own financial gain?
  <br><br>
  That's the claim made by a potential class of academics and researchers in an antitrust suit filed last week in Brooklyn against six major academic journal publishers: Elsevier, Wolters Kluwer, Wiley, Sage Publications, Taylor &amp; Francis, and Springer Nature.
