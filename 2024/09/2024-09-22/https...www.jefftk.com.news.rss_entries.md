## Becket First

 - RSS feed: $source

<p><span>

One of the things I like most about contra dance is how it works with
a very wide range of skill levels, including having one of the best
learning curves of any dance form I know.  As a community we are
pretty committed to the idea that people should just be able to show
up and start dancing, no lessons required. Which is why I'm very
excited about a new "Becket first" approach I've been seeing from
several callers.

</span>

<p>

Contra dance has several common starting formations, which are
traditionally taught as:

</p>

<p>

</p>

<ul>

<li><p>Proper: one line of Larks, one line of Robins.  It's "proper"
because everyone is on their own ("proper") side.

</p></li>
<li><p>Improper: start proper, then within each couple that is going
to be going down the hall ("active" or "ones") the Lark and Robin
swap.

</p></li>
<li><p>Becket: start improper, then circle one place to the left.

</p></li>
</ul>
