<p>With a big-time director and screenwriter like Roland Emmerich (Independence Day, Godzilla, Stargate) and mega-stars like Anthony Hopkins (Silence of the Lambs, Thor) and Iwan Rheon (Game of Thrones, Misfits) attached, Those About to Die was billed as Game of Thrones with togas and had an audience waiting to be thrilled. Set during a period of political intrigue in the Roman Empire, could the expensive TV series live up to its own hype? The Romans have given writers ample inspiration to create thrilling stories and interesting characters over the years. We don’t have too long to wait before a sequel to one of the greatest movies ever made, Gladiator, hits the big screen but to fill the gap before then, we have Those About to Die. Rome in the first Century CE is like a viper’s den. Emperor Vespasian (an under-used Hopkins) has a choice to make as he decides which of his sons deserves to take over from him – his well-respected soldier son Titus, or the wily politician, Domitian.
