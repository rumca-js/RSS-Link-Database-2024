The smiling statue of the god Vishnu will undergo a thorough analysis, as well as a slight facelift, by researchers between its May departure from Cambodia and the opening of Paris' Guimet Museum of Asian Arts' exhibition on Khmer bronzes on April 30, 2025.

## Inside the foundry of Angkor's kings
 - [https://www.lemonde.fr/en/science/article/2024/04/21/inside-the-foundry-of-angkor-s-kings_6669084_10.html](https://www.lemonde.fr/en/science/article/2024/04/21/inside-the-foundry-of-angkor-s-kings_6669084_10.html)
 - RSS feed: https://www.lemonde.fr/en/science/rss_full.xml
 - date published: 2024-04-21T15:00:22+00:00

An archaeometallurgical study of the royal palace of Angkor's foundry, dating back to the 11th century, began in 2016 as part of a Franco-Cambodian research project. Little by little, the site has revealed the secrets of the bronzesmiths' craft. Archaeologists have also traced the metal back to ancient copper mines.
