FromSoftware has rightfully established itself as one of the top names in the industry, as it continues to pump out innovative releases year after year. But even amongst this vast buffet of commendable releases, some tend to stand out more than others, in this case 2019’s Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice. 

It’s been more than 5 years since it was originally released, but it continues to be one of the best action games of recent memory with an extremely polished combat loop and a well realized Sengoku Japan setting. With this video, we take a look back at the game after all these years and try to break down what exactly Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice did to achieve such a timeless nature.

It can be challenging to build a franchise - to launch a critically acclaimed forerunner and follow it up with hit after hit. 

While there are others with excellent track records, from Ninja Gaiden and Mass Effect to Dead Rising and Silent Hill, they inevitably stumble into the abyss. Call it development troubles, not understanding what made the franchise such a success, reinvention gone wrong, lack of resources - the list goes on.

Either way, these franchises go from good to terrible seemingly overnight with no explanation. Let's look at 15 such examples, their current states in today's world and, in some cases, if there's any hope of a comeback.
