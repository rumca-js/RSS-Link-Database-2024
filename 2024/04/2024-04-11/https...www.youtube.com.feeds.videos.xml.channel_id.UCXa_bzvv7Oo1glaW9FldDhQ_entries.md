There are certain mechanics and quality of life features that we all take for granted in most open world games. Fast travel, for instance, is deemed almost a necessity in the vast majority of games that fall in that category (and even many that do not)- but it is perhaps always not as necessary as one might think. 

Either through the strength of their world design, the visual beauty of their map, their excellent traversal mechanics, or any number of other factors, games have often found a way to make navigation and exploration so engaging that fast travel almost ends up feeling unnecessary. Here, we are going to take a look at a few games that manage to do just that.
