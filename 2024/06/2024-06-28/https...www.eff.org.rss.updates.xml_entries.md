## Mississippi Can’t Wall Off Everyone’s Social Media Access to Protect Children
 - [https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/06/mississippi-cant-wall-everyones-social-media-access-protect-children](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/06/mississippi-cant-wall-everyones-social-media-access-protect-children)
 - RSS feed: https://www.eff.org/rss/updates.xml
 - date published: 2024-06-28T16:48:14+00:00

<div class="field field--name-body field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden"><div class="field__items"><div class="field__item even"><p>In what is becoming <a href="https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2023/10/your-states-child-safety-law-unconstitutional-try-comprehensive-data-privacy">a recurring theme</a>, Mississippi became the latest state to pass a law requiring social media services to verify users’ ages and block lawful speech to young people. Once again, EFF explained to the court why the law is unconstitutional.</p>
<p>Mississippi’s law (<a href="https://billstatus.ls.state.ms.us/documents/2024/pdf/HB/1100-1199/HB1126SG.pdf">House Bill 1126</a>) requires social media services to verify the ages of all users, to obtain parental consent for any minor users, and to block minor users from being exposed to “harmful” material. NetChoice, the trade association that represents some of the largest social media services, filed suit and sought to block the law from going into effect
