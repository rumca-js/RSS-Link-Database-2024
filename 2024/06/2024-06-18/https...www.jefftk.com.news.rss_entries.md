<p>

<i>Summary: the watches do the basic things they need to and have let
us give our kids more freedom, but there are also a bunch of annoying
limitations and they're 36% more expensive than they look. I'm overall
happy we got them.</i>

</p>

<p>

Our older two kids, Lily and Anna, are ten and eight, and are mature
enough that they're able to cross streets and handle unusual
situations. They can go to the park or a nearby friends house <a href="https://www.jefftk.com/p/kids-roaming">on their own</a>, but we
do need to know where they are.  Over the past <a href="https://www.jefftk.com/p/walkie-talkies">three years</a> we've
mostly used three strategies.  They can:

</p>

<p>

</p>

<ul>

<li><p>Tell us where they're going, and if they want to go
somewhere else they need to come home and tell us first.

</p></li>
<li><p>Bring a <a href="https://www.jefftk.com/p/walkie-talkies">walkie-talkie</a>.

</p></li>
<li><p>Get a friend's parent to text or call us.
