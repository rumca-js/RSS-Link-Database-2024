<p>With Daughter of Calamity, Rosalie M. Lin has written one of the most interesting debuts of the year (see our review here). Her work is nuanced and far darker than the cover would suggest. Whisking the reader away to the Shanghai of the 1930s, she introduces us to organised crime, body parts as a commodity and cultural appropriation. It was great to catch up with her ahead of publication and we&#8217;re already keen for her next book. [GdM] Can you introduce Daughter of Calamity in one or two sentences for our readers? [RML] Daughter of Calamity is a historical fantasy set in 1930s Shanghai about a cabaret dancer named Jingwen who discovers a secret trade of dancers’ faces and decides to fight back. The story has silver-armed gangsters, petty showgirls with hearts of gold, and ancient gods that can be worn and traded like furs. [GdM] I loved the book’s setting.
