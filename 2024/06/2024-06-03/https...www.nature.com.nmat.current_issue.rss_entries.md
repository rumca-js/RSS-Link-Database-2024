## Evidence for electron–hole crystals in a Mott insulator
 - [https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01910-3](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01910-3)
 - RSS feed: https://www.nature.com/nmat/current_issue/rss
 - date published: 2024-06-03T09:55:36.423751+00:00

<p>Nature Materials, Published online: 03 June 2024; <a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-024-01910-3">doi:10.1038/s41563-024-01910-3</a></p>Scanning tunnelling microscopy of doped RuCl3 shows distinct charge orderings at the lower and upper Hubbard bands, which can be attributed to a correlation-driven honeycomb hole crystal composed of hole-rich Ru sites and a rotational-symmetry-breaking paired electron crystal composed of electron-rich Ru–Ru bonds.
